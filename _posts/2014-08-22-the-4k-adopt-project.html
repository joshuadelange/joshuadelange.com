The 4k adopt project

Published on August 22nd, 2014

Since 1960 YWAM has been sending missionary teams to places all over the world to tell everyone about Jesus, make people’s lives better and train them to be more awesome at engaging with society.

Because YWAM takes ‘going to the ends of the earth’ pretty seriously, YWAM has been keeping track of what countries they went to in order to make sure YWAM went everywhere and see where there is room for geographical expansion.

This was all great and amazing, but then during the 90’s YWAM went to the last country on earth, they had a little ‘um, …now what?’ moment.

4k and the Omega Zones

(also: good band name)

Then one day, the brilliant concept of ‘Omega Zones’ was created. Instead of of just checking off countries, the world would be split up based off population instead of only existing geopolitical boundaries.

Without Omega Zones:

India: check!

With Omega Zones:

ohnvm.

“The world's established boundaries aren’t good enough for us; lets make our own!”

Such a ywam-y thing to say.

A magical formula was released upon the world and we ended up with four thousand of these Omega Zones; therefore, the framework would be called 4k.

Good stuff. The 4k framework has been around for a while and has been used as a common language between major missions organizations. But now, it’s all about to get Omega Zone crazy over here.

4k adopt

In a few weeks, hundreds of YWAM leaders from around the world will come together to strategize, collaborate and dream together on how to be even better at what we do.

One of the things that will be implemented will be a project we’re calling ‘4k Adopt’. The idea is that all these YWAM leaders will ‘adopt’ Omega Zones. With this adoption, they’re saying they'll send teams there, start activities there or in any way engage that region with the work they’re doing.

The cool thing is that they’re shooting for adopting all 4000+ Omega Zones by the year 2020. (For those passionate about changing our world, setting a vision for the year 2020 is the thing to do these days)

Having hundreds of missionary leaders strategize and collaborate on how to impact every single region of the world by the year 2020 is pretty significant. But, you may wonder, how does that all work out logistically?

And that’s where 4k team comes in.

Artist rendition of the global '4k team'

Our mission, if we would choose to accept it (and we totally did);

Enable hundreds of leaders within a limited time together commit to 4000+ regions of the world and give them the ability to see each others activity as they go.

So here’s the recipe for awesomeness we came up with;

  • A huge floor map for people to walk over with, get a good overview of the situation.

    Created by the one and only map queen of missions; Jill Thornton (she’s kinda good at these floor maps)

  • Mobile apps for Android and iOS for people to indicate they’re adopting the Omega Zone they’re walking on and by what year they expect to be there.

    Built out by the fearless Min Seong Kang and his intimidating army of Korean coders.

  • A realtime updating map with a sweet looking countdown and live stream of activity.

    Together with the above mentioned fearless Min Seong Kang, I had the great pleasure of building out something pretty.

Le data visualization.

People will walk over the map, adopt the Omega Zone on their phone and see their Omega Zone turn red on the big screen. I think geographic data visualizations aren’t just a ‘nice thing to have’. I believe they’re crucial for people to understand what is happening and get them thinking on how to get involved.

With all of this hard work, we hope to have to created tools for missions leaders worldwide to strategize together on how to reach that last people group with Jesus’ love, care for those who live in the margins of societies anywhere in the world and teach those societies on how to structure themselves Gods way.

The meeting is in a few weeks. I’ll let you know how it went!

And now for some yummy technical details!

Technically, this project was super interesting! Throwing 4000+ polygons in a web browser is always asking for performance challenges, but we’ve been working hard to minimize those and I feel good about the place we’ve been able to take it to.

Optimizing tiled polygons, vector rendering and more of that good stuff was awesome to work on. It also was the first time for me working with web sockets to make sure the map would realtime update with the least performance overhead as possible.

Technology used;

  • HTML, CSS, Javascript (Obviously)
  • Mapping libraries; leaflet.js + esri-leaflet.js
  • Omega Zone polygon data API; ArcGIS Online
  • Adoption data API; Django + Django REST framework (mostly done by Min)
  • Websockets magic; Custom built NodeJS server

Check out the Github repo for the code: https://github.com/4kmapping/4kadopt_server
